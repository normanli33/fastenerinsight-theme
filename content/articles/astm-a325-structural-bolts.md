# ASTM A325: The Classic Structural Bolt Standard, Explained

Ask anyone in steel construction to name a bolt standard and **ASTM A325** is usually the first answer. For decades it defined the heavy hex structural bolt used in buildings, bridges, and heavy steel connections across North America — and its designation still appears on drawings, RFQs, and bolt heads today. Here's what the standard actually covers, and one important update every specifier should know.

## What ASTM A325 Covers

ASTM A325 is the specification for **quenched and tempered (heat treated) steel structural bolts** with a heavy hex head, produced in imperial sizes from **1/2" to 1-1/2" diameter**. The mechanical requirements are the heart of the spec:

- **120 ksi minimum tensile strength** for diameters 1" and under
- **105 ksi minimum tensile strength** for diameters over 1" up to 1-1/2"

The heavy hex head and short thread length distinguish A325 bolts from ordinary hex cap screws — the geometry follows the heavy hex structural bolt dimensional standard, giving a larger bearing surface and more unthreaded shank in the connection's shear plane.

## Types Within the Standard

| Type | Material | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| Type 1 | Medium carbon or carbon boron steel | General structural connections; can be hot-dip galvanized |
| Type 3 | Weathering steel | Unpainted weathering steel structures (matches corrosion behaviour of the parent steel) |

**Type 2 was withdrawn** decades ago, so current product is Type 1 or Type 3 only.

## The Assembly, Not Just the Bolt

A325 bolts are almost never supplied alone. A structural bolting assembly pairs the bolt with:

- **ASTM A563 heavy hex nuts** (commonly grade C or DH)
- **ASTM F436 hardened washers**
- Optionally, **F959 direct tension indicators** for pretension verification

Installation methods — snug-tight, turn-of-nut, calibrated wrench, or tension-control — depend on whether the connection is bearing-type or slip-critical, so the joint design drives the procurement requirement, not the bolt grade alone.

## Important: A325 Is Now ASTM F3125 Grade A325

In 2016, ASTM consolidated its structural bolt specifications. A325, A490, F1852, and F2280 were withdrawn and rolled into a single umbrella standard: **ASTM F3125**. The familiar grade lives on as **F3125 Grade A325** — same mechanical requirements, same head marking, same applications.

**What this means for buyers:** current mill certificates will reference F3125 Grade A325. If your customer's drawing calls out "ASTM A325" alone, product certified to F3125 Grade A325 is the correct, compliant supply — but confirm the documentation wording at order stage to avoid disputes at goods receipt.

## Metric and International Equivalents

The metric companion (A325M, ~830 MPa class) broadly corresponds to **property class 8.8 high-strength structural bolting**. In European projects, the comparable system is **EN 14399** (HR assemblies, historically DIN 6914 bolts with DIN 6915 nuts and DIN 6916 washers) — similar in intent, but not a drop-in substitution without engineering approval.

## The Bottom Line

ASTM A325 remains the shorthand for the standard North American high-strength structural bolt: heavy hex, heat treated, 120/105 ksi. Just remember that the paperwork has moved on — specify or accept **F3125 Grade A325**, order it as a matched assembly with the right nuts and washers, and align certification wording before the truck leaves the supplier.
