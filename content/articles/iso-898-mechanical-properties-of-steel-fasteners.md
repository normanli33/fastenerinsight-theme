# ISO 898 — Mechanical Properties of Steel Fasteners

**ISO 898** is a family of international standards defining the **mechanical and physical properties of carbon-steel and alloy-steel fasteners**. It establishes property classes such as **4.6, 8.8, 10.9 and 12.9** for bolts and **Class 8, 10 and 12** for nuts.

It mainly answers:

- How strong must the fastener be?
- What material and heat-treatment requirements apply?
- What proof, tensile and hardness tests are required?
- How should the fastener be marked?

ISO 898 does **not normally define the dimensions** of the bolt or nut. Dimensions are controlled by product standards such as ISO 4014, ISO 4017, ISO 4032 or DIN equivalents.

## Main Parts

| Standard | Scope |
|---|---|
| **ISO 898-1** | Bolts, screws and studs made from carbon or alloy steel |
| **ISO 898-2** | Nuts made from non-alloy or alloy steel |
| **ISO 898-3** | Flat washers used with property-class fasteners |
| **ISO 898-7** | Torsional testing and minimum torque for small bolts and screws |

## ISO 898-1: Bolt Property Classes

A bolt property class contains two numbers, for example:

> **8.8**

The first number indicates the nominal tensile strength:

    8 × 100 = 800 MPa

The second number indicates the ratio between nominal yield strength and tensile strength:

    0.8 × 800 = 640 MPa

Therefore, an **8.8 bolt** has approximately:

- Nominal tensile strength: **800 MPa**
- Nominal yield strength: **640 MPa**

### Common Property Classes

| Property class | Nominal tensile strength | Nominal yield strength or 0.2% proof stress | General description |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| 4.6 | 400 MPa | 240 MPa | Low-strength mild steel |
| 4.8 | 400 MPa | 320 MPa | Low-strength commercial fastener |
| 5.6 | 500 MPa | 300 MPa | Medium-strength steel |
| 5.8 | 500 MPa | 400 MPa | General engineering |
| 6.8 | 600 MPa | 480 MPa | Medium-strength fastener |
| 8.8 | 800 MPa | 640 MPa | High-strength structural and engineering bolt |
| 9.8 | 900 MPa | 720 MPa | Often used in smaller diameters |
| 10.9 | 1,000 MPa | 900 MPa | High-strength, normally quenched and tempered |
| 12.9 | 1,200 MPa | 1,080 MPa | Very-high-strength socket screws and specialised applications |

The exact minimum values can vary slightly depending on diameter, manufacturing method and the applicable table in ISO 898-1.

ISO 898-1 applies to carbon- and alloy-steel bolts, screws and studs tested under standard laboratory conditions. The stated mechanical properties may not be retained at very high or very low service temperatures.

## ISO 898-2: Nut Property Classes

Nuts use a single-number property class, such as:

> **Class 8 nut**

The class number broadly corresponds to the highest bolt property class with which the nut is intended to be paired under the standard&rsquo;s specified conditions.

Typical combinations are:

| Bolt property class | Common corresponding nut class |
|---|---|
| 4.6, 4.8, 5.6 or 5.8 | Class 5 or higher, depending on the joint |
| 8.8 | Class 8 |
| 10.9 | Class 10 |
| 12.9 | Class 12 |

A **Class 8 nut** is not described as &ldquo;8.8.&rdquo; The correct designation is:

- Bolt: **Property Class 8.8**
- Nut: **Property Class 8**

Nut performance is principally verified by a **proof-load test**, because the nut must support the specified load without thread stripping or unacceptable permanent deformation.

## Important Tests and Requirements

ISO 898 includes requirements for matters such as:

- Tensile strength
- Yield strength or proof stress
- Proof load
- Elongation after fracture
- Reduction of area
- Hardness
- Impact strength for applicable classes and sizes
- Decarburisation and carburisation
- Head soundness
- Wedge tensile testing
- Torsional strength for certain small fasteners
- Chemical composition and heat treatment
- Surface integrity

For high-strength classes such as **8.8, 10.9 and 12.9**, proper material selection and heat treatment are critical.

## Marking Examples

A standard hex bolt may be marked:

> **10.9 XYZ**

Where:

- **10.9** = property class
- **XYZ** = manufacturer&rsquo;s identification mark

A nut may be marked:

> **10 XYZ**

Marking requirements depend on the fastener type, size and available marking space.

## What ISO 898 Does Not Cover

ISO 898 is generally not the appropriate mechanical-property standard for:

- Stainless-steel fasteners &mdash; commonly covered by ISO 3506
- Non-ferrous fasteners
- Fasteners requiring special corrosion resistance
- Fasteners intended for very high or cryogenic temperatures
- Weldable fasteners requiring special material control
- Thread-forming or self-tapping screws, unless specifically covered elsewhere
- Fasteners whose principal loading is outside the standard&rsquo;s defined scope

## Practical Specification Examples

A properly described product might be:

> **M16 &times; 60 hexagon head bolt to ISO 4014, steel property class 8.8 to ISO 898-1, zinc plated.**

For a nut:

> **M16 hexagon nut to ISO 4032, steel property class 8 to ISO 898-2, zinc plated.**

Here:

- **ISO 4014 / ISO 4032** controls the dimensions.
- **ISO 898-1 / ISO 898-2** controls the mechanical properties.
- The coating specification controls corrosion protection.

The dimensional standard, mechanical-property standard, thread tolerance and coating requirement should therefore all be stated separately.
